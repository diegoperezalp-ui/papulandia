Conectores de Tiempo: WHEN y WHILE

Domina la diferencia entre interrupciones y acciones paralelas en el pasado.

1. Reglas Generales de WHEN

Se usa para unir una acción larga (Past Continuous) con una acción corta (Past Simple) que la interrumpe.

Past Continuous + WHEN + Past Simple

Ejemplos:

  • I was having dinner when you called.
  • The lights went out when we were watching the movie.
  • She was driving when it started to rain.

2. Reglas Generales de WHILE

Se usa exclusivamente para dos acciones largas que ocurren simultáneamente en el pasado.

Past Continuous + WHILE + Past Continuous

Ejemplos:

  • While I was studying, my brother was playing video games.
  • The sun was shining while the birds were singing.
  • While she was cooking, he was cleaning the house.

3. Resumen Rápido: ¿Cuándo usar cada uno?

WHEN

Interrupción

Acción corta que corta una larga

I was sleeping when the alarm rang.

WHILE

Simultáneo

Dos acciones largas al mismo tiempo

While I was cooking, she was reading.

Truco Mnemotécnico (¡Nunca lo olvidarás!)

WHILE → siempre lleva verbo en -ING después

W-H-I-L-E tiene 5 letras → como el -ING
