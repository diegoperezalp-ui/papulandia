When vs While – La Guía Definitiva con Reglas Avanzadas

WHEN = 1 acción corta interrumpe (Past Simple)
WHILE = 2 acciones largas al mismo tiempo (Past Continuous)

Primero: ¿Qué es el Past Continuous?

Forma: was/were + verbo-ing
Ej.: I was eating / They were playing

Negativa: was/were not (wasn't/weren't)
Pregunta: Was/Were + sujeto + verbo-ing?
Ej.: Was she sleeping? / Weren't they watching TV?
Verbos no continuos (stative): No uses Past Continuous con verbos de estado como "know", "like", "have" (posesión). Usa Past Simple.
✗ I was knowing the answer. → ✓ I knew the answer.

1. WHEN → Interrupción (acción corta)

Estructura común:
Past Continuous + when + Past Simple (interrupción repentina)

Acción larga ← when → Acción corta (¡PUM! interrupción)

  • I was cooking when the phone rang.
  • She was driving when she saw the accident.
  • We were watching TV when the lights went out.
  • He was playing football when he broke his arm.

Uso avanzado: Para "fondo de historia" (set the scene). Ej.: The sun was shining when the adventure began.

Truco mental: "¿Qué pasó de repente?" → WHEN. La acción corta es como un "punto" en la línea del tiempo.

Pega aquí: alguien cocinando + teléfono sonando

2. WHILE → Simultáneo (dos acciones largas)

Estructura común:
Past Continuous + while + Past Continuous (acciones paralelas)

Acción larga ← while → Otra acción larga (paralelas, como dos líneas)

  • While I was studying, my sister was playing games.
  • They were talking while the teacher was explaining.
  • While we were walking, we were listening to music.
  • While she was cooking, he was watching TV.

Uso avanzado: Para acciones temporales o irritantes. Ej.: He was always complaining while I was trying to work.

Truco mental: "¿Qué más estaba pasando al mismo tiempo?" → WHILE. Enfatiza duración, no interrupción.

Pega aquí: dos personas haciendo cosas distintas al mismo tiempo

Diferencias Sutiles: WHEN vs WHILE

Énfasis: "When" es neutral; "while" enfatiza simultaneidad o duración.
Ej.: I was studying when he arrived. (Hecho simple)
I was studying while he arrived. (Enfocado en el proceso de estudio)

Puntuación: Coma si when/while va al inicio: While I was cooking, the phone rang.
Sin coma si va en medio: I was cooking when the phone rang.
Frases clave: at 5 PM, all day, when/while it was raining, during the storm.

Resumen Visual (¡Grábatelo!)

Línea del tiempo: WHEN = punto de interrupción | WHILE = dos líneas paralelas

Errores Comunes (¡Evítalos!)

✗ While the phone rang, I was cooking. → Mal (while para corta)

✓ When the phone rang, I was cooking. → Correcto

✗ I was sleeping when I was hearing a noise. → Mal (stative verb)

✓ I was sleeping when I heard a noise. → Correcto

✗ When I was studying, I was listening to music. → Raro (dos continuas con when)

✓ While I was studying, I was listening to music. → Natural

✗ I cook dinner when I arrived. → Mal (falta continuous)

✓ I was cooking dinner when I arrived. → Correcto
